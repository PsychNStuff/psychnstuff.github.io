Outreach

As someone who values connecting with peers, disseminating scientific information to the community, and enjoys helping others, I have consistently done my best to continue doing all of these things in ways that are both safe and appropriate. I have tried to understand others' diverse perspectives, even if I disagree with them personally.

Open Science and Science Communication

Open science includes sharing papers, methods, data, and code, as well as communicating findings to fellow scientists at conferences or via social media and clearly communicating information with non-scientists. In order to reach my peers, I use the Open Science Framework for preregistration of data and to add information about methods as well as GitHub on which I share open source code. I use Twitter, LinkedIn, and more recently, Mastodon, as ways to network with others more informally. I have also had the opportunity to attend conferences, often due to the fact that they were available online due to the COVID-19 pandemic. More recently, however, I have begun attending conferences in-person.

Community Service and Volunteering

Serving the community at-large is an incredible way to help others, and research has shown that this has psychological benefits. In the past, I have volunteered with a virtual texting helpline called 7 Cups of Tea. During the pandemic, this allowed me to help others from the relative safety of my home. As hospitals began accepting new volunteers, I connected with and began volunteering at a large trauma hospital near me called HonorHealth in March, 2022. Since then, I have been volunteering once a week at the hospital by answering questions, providing direction, and doing necessary tasks such as restocking masks. My most recent endeavor has been at a nonprofit called Letters to a Pre-Scientist focused on making scientists seem human to youth in middle school. I have been matched with a pre-scientist who is my penpal, and am excited to write letters back and forth with this student for the 2022-2023 school year!

Leadership and Advocacy

Throughout high school and my undergraduate training, I sought leadership positions. I was part of the Next Generation Service Corps, was the Director of Community Outreach for Psi Chi at Arizona State University, and was a member of multiple boards and committees. However, it is now my personal belief that leadership comes from listening and learning, and leading by example. Although I do not currently hold any leadership positions as I have graduated, I am lucky to be a member of the American Psychological Association and Psi Chi, the International Honors Society in Psychology.
